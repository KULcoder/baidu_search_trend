COVID: A Search Trend

By Keyu Long

We don't always need direct data to gauge the situation. When the People's Republic of China1 eased the nationwide lockdown policy at the end of 2022, they also ceased the release of daily COVID-19 data. Nonetheless, we can still get a sense of the situation by examining the search trends on Baidu, China's most popular search engine.

Geographical Representation

Observe the Baidu search trend1 for the term fever across different provinces2 from November 2022 to February 2023.

Date: 11/1/2022

Trends over Different Words

Different words can describe the situation from different perspectives. We will explore search volumn3 of fever, cough, headache, insomnia, and ICU in whole nation over time.

Relationships between Provinces

With the trending data of searching fever of different provinces, we can find the relationship4 between them.

1. Log scale is used to better show the differences between provinces.
2. Mainland China is considered, excluding Taiwan, Hong Kong, and Macao as they have different COVID-19 policies.
3. In addition to raw data, standardized data is calculated using the z-score standardization method for the search volume of each keyword.
4. The relationships are calculated by the correlation coefficient of the search trends in different provinces.
